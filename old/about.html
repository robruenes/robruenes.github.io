About Me

I grew up in Centereach, New York, a hamlet in Suffolk County, Long Island, just over an hour east of New York City, living with my wonderful parents, sister, and a number of different dogs over the years. While living on the island, I cultivated strong interests in music, fencing epee, and the sciences. When I wasn't competing or studying, you would probably find me with my friends in one of the city's many music venues.

I graduated from Centereach High School in 2011, ranked as class Salutatorian and a member of three academic honor societies. I entered Tufts University that fall, believing that I would study Biology and later attend medical school, until I took Introduction to Computer Science with Remco Chang (now my advisor), and realized that I wanted to major in CS. Since then, I have both taken and TA'd for numerous computer science courses here at Tufts.

If I'm not at work in Halligan Hall, you can probably find me in the Jackson Gym, leading the Tufts Men's Fencing Team's epee squad as captain, or practicing bass in the late hours of the night, much to the chagrin of my housemates.
